What is ATS?

ATS is a communication service like Discord, but without the moderation, privacy invasions, or questionable peer-to-peer chat setups. ATS doesn’t care who you are, where you're from, what you think. ATS hate KYC (Know Your Customer), no identity checks, ever.

Is ATS Safe?

How safe is ATS? That’s up to you to decide, but here’s what ATS know:
[1] ATS is open-source.
[2] It’s only available through a .onion (Tor) address, ATS doesn’t want your IP.
[3] When you sign up, you're given a private key and a public key.

Your Private Key
Your private key is like your username and password combined. It’s how you log in, KEEP IT A SECRET!

Your Public Key
Your public Key is your "Handle". One ATS user can use your public key to send you a connection request.
Once someone uses it, you get to accept or decline the request. No matter what you choose, that public key will selfdestruct and a new public key is created helping you stay anonymous and untraceable. YOU CAN SHARE THIS TO OTHERS!

What Does ATS Store?

Almost nothing. You store your own chat logs on your device. ATS only uses keys to help connect users. No logs, no surveillance.
